LU-SGS

Jatinder Pal Singh Sandhu
Sep 19, 2018 1 min read CFD

Lower Upper Symmertic Gauss Sidel method is matrix free implicit time-integration scheme. If we spatilly discretize any governing eqution; using any one of the well known methods: Finite difference method, Finite Volume method, Finite element method, etc; we as left with system of odinary differential equations. $$V\frac{dU_i}{dt} = R_i^{n+1}$$ Where, “$i$” represents the cell center or cell node point based on the spatial discrtization method. U and R are the state and residual vector stored at spatial point “$i$”. $V$ represents the volume of the cell. To integrate this system of ODE we can use Euler time integration scheme: $$V\frac{\Delta U^n_i}{\Delta t} = R_i^{n+1}$$ Where $\Delta t$ is the time increment and $\Delta U^n_i$ represents the difference between state vector at time level $n+1$ and $n$: $U^{n+1}_i - U^n_i$. Since $R^{n+1}_i$ is unkonw, we linearize the above equation to approximate unknown quantity, $R^{n+1}_i$ in terms of known quantities. $$V\frac{\Delta U^n_i}{\Delta t} = R_i^{n} + \left(\frac{\partial R}{\partial U}\right)^n\Delta U_i^n$$ Rearranging the above equation to combine all the $\Delta U^n_i$ terms: $$\left(\frac{V}{\Delta t} + \left(\frac{\partial R}{\partial U}\right)^n\right)\Delta U_i^n = R_i^{n}$$
